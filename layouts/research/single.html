Research

My research investigates the behavioral consequences of digital systems — specifically, how the design of online platforms, social networks, and AI-powered tools shapes human cognition, decision-making, and social dynamics in ways that are frequently unintended. I study these phenomena through three interlocking research streams, combining behavioral experimentation, econometric analysis, and computational methods.

Current Direction

Investigating how generative AI reshapes human decision-making, creative behavior, and social polarization. Active projects include: how algorithmic transparency shapes content creator behavior (under review); how AI can bridge social divides (AI Initiatives Grant, William & Mary 2024); and contrarian choice behavior in online marketplaces — examining why consumers systematically deviate from crowd recommendations.
